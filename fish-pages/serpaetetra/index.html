Serpae Tetra

The Serpae Tetra (Hyphessobrycon eques), also known as the Red Minor Tetra or Jewel Tetra, is a vibrant and striking freshwater fish native to the rivers and streams of South America, particularly in the Amazon basin.
The most notable feature of the Serpae Tetra is its deep red body and a prominent black spot, often referred to as a "false eye," located just behind the gill cover. This spot, combined with the intense red color, gives the fish a distinctive and eye-catching appearance.
Serpae Tetras are omnivores and will accept a variety of foods. A balanced diet should include high-quality flake or pellet food, supplemented with live or frozen foods such as brine shrimp, daphnia, and bloodworms. This varied diet helps to maintain their vibrant colors and overall health.
While Serpae Tetras are generally peaceful, they can sometimes display aggressive behavior, especially towards smaller or slower-moving fish. They are best kept with other peaceful, similarly sized fish that can handle a bit of playful chasing. Good tank mates include other tetras, small barbs, danios, and peaceful bottom-dwelling species like corydoras catfish.
